I am biologist interested in studying and applying biological and ecological theory in conservation biology and urban ecosystems. 

I am currently a PhD Candidate in the Biology Department at Carleton University, supervised by Dr. Lenore Fahrig. My research interests lie at the intersection of urban landscape ecology and conservation biology, focusing on the impacts of urban landscape configuration, such as the connectivity of greenspaces, on biodiversity and ecosystem functions. I am particularly interested in understanding how urban landscapes can be planned and managed to support biodiversity, enhance ecological connectivity, and provide ecosystem services for both humans and wildlife. My work involves using advanced spatial analysis and modeling techniques to assess the status and priorities for biodiversity-inclusive urban connectivity planning.

Through my research, I aim to develop strategies that mitigate the negative effects of continued urban sprawl on increasingly fragmented natural habitats while promoting sustainable urban planning. This includes studying green infrastructure, such as urban forests and green roofs, and their role in providing habitat, facilitating dispersal, and conserving biodiversity across species, populations, and communities.

If if you are interested in urban ecology, urban landscapes, and conservation biology, I would love to hear from you. Please contact me to discuss any ongoing or potential future research, projects, papers, or collaborations!
